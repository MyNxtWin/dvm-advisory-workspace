SOURCE: DVM Internal Compliance Practice Note — HR (Updated: FY 2025–26)

SECTION 1: STATUTORY REGISTRATIONS FOR EMPLOYERS

MANDATORY REGISTRATIONS BY THRESHOLD:

1. PROVIDENT FUND (EPF) — Threshold: 20+ employees
   - Employer contribution: 12% of basic + DA
   - Employee contribution: 12% of basic + DA
   - Employer additional: 0.5% (EDLI insurance) + admin charges ~1.1%
   - Registration: EPFO online portal within 30 days of crossing threshold
   - Filing: ECR (Electronic Challan cum Return) — monthly by 15th
   - Annual return: Form 3A and 6A by April 30
   - UAN mandatory for every employee; Aadhaar seeding required

2. EMPLOYEES STATE INSURANCE (ESI) — Threshold: 10+ employees (20 in some states)
   - Applicable: employees earning ≤₹21,000/month gross
   - Employer contribution: 3.25% of gross wages
   - Employee contribution: 0.75% of gross wages
   - Registration: ESIC portal within 15 days of applicability
   - Filing: half-yearly returns (April–September, October–March)
   - Benefits: medical, maternity, disability, dependent benefits

3. PROFESSIONAL TAX — State-specific
   - Maharashtra: slab up to ₹2,500/year per employee (employer deducts from salary)
   - Karnataka: up to ₹2,400/year
   - Employer PT registration separate from employee PT
   - Filing frequency: varies by state (monthly/annual)

4. SHOPS & ESTABLISHMENTS ACT — All commercial establishments
   - Register with local municipal authority within 30 days of commencing business
   - Renew annually (state-specific)
   - Governs: working hours, weekly off, holidays, leave entitlements
   - Display: registration certificate must be prominently displayed at workplace

5. LABOUR WELFARE FUND — State-specific
   - Applicable states include: Maharashtra, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Gujarat, Telangana
   - Contribution: nominal (typically ₹6–₹36/year per employee depending on state)
   - Deducted from June and December salaries

SECTION 2: LEAVE ENTITLEMENTS (COMMERCIAL ESTABLISHMENTS)

TYPE | ENTITLEMENT | NOTES
Earned Leave (EL) | Typically 15–21 days per year | Accrual-based; usually 1 day per 20 days worked; encashable
Casual Leave (CL) | 6–12 days per year | Non-accumulating; lapses at year end
Sick Leave (SL) | 6–12 days per year | Medical certificate may be required >3 consecutive days
Maternity Leave | 26 weeks (if ≤2 surviving children) / 12 weeks thereafter | Maternity Benefit Act, 1961; paid; applicable where 10+ employees
Paternity Leave | No statutory right — policy-based | Best practice: 5–10 days
National Holidays | 3 mandatory (Republic Day, Independence Day, Gandhi Jayanti) | Balance fixed by state
Festival Holidays | Varies by state | Shops Act calendar

LEAVE ENCASHMENT ON SEPARATION:
- Earned leave accumulated at date of separation must be encashed
- Calculation: (Monthly basic + DA) / 26 × number of EL days pending
- Tax: exempt up to ₹3,00,000 for non-government employees (Section 10(10AA))

LEAVE POLICY BEST PRACTICE FOR PROFESSIONAL FIRMS:
- Cap EL accumulation at 45 days to limit liability
- Allow carry-forward of max 15 days per year
- Encashment during service: permitted once per year, max 10 days

SECTION 3: GRATUITY (PAYMENT OF GRATUITY ACT, 1972)

APPLICABILITY: 10+ employees (once applicable, continues even if headcount falls)
ELIGIBILITY: 5 years of continuous service (4 years + 240 days in 5th year for daily-wage workers; court decisions vary on contract staff)
FORMULA: (Last drawn basic + DA) / 26 × 15 × number of years of service
CAP: ₹20,00,000 (as amended; enhanced from ₹10,00,000)
PAYMENT TIMELINE: Within 30 days of becoming payable
TAX: Exempt up to ₹20,00,000 for private sector employees

FUNDING OPTIONS:
- Pay as you go: settle on separation (smallest firms, cash flow risk)
- Group gratuity scheme: LIC or private insurer (premium deductible under IT Act)
- Gratuity trust: approved trust, actuarial valuation required annually

NOTE: Nomination form must be obtained from every employee within 30 days of joining.

SECTION 4: CONTRACT STAFFING & GIG ENGAGEMENT

FIXED-TERM EMPLOYEES (FTE):
- Labour Codes allow fixed-term contracts for any role (not just seasonal)
- FTE entitled to all statutory benefits including gratuity (pro-rated for <5 years)
- No deemed permanency even on renewal — distinguishes from contract workmen
- Use case: project-specific filings, one-time transaction support

CONSULTANTS / RETAINERS (Section 194J TDS):
- Engage via consultancy agreement; not an employment contract
- TDS: 10% on professional fees (2% if turnover <₹1 Cr)
- GST: consultant must register if turnover >₹20L (₹10L for some states); firm deducts TDS, consultant charges GST
- Benefit: no EPF/ESI obligation on firm (verify: courts have questioned this for economic-dependence situations)
- Risk: if court finds disguised employment → all statutory dues become payable retrospectively

INTERN / TRAINEE ENGAGEMENT:
- CS trainees: governed by ICSI Training Guidelines; stipend not wages for PF purposes
- CA articleship: ICAI norms; stipend not subject to PF/ESI typically
- Other interns: if >₹21,000/month stipend, ESI applicability examined; PF: EPF office has issued notices to firms treating interns as employees

SECTION 5: TERMINATION & EXIT COMPLIANCE

NOTICE PERIOD:
- Statutory minimum: 30 days for establishments with 100+ workmen (Industrial Disputes Act)
- For managerial staff: contractual notice period prevails (not statutory minimum)
- Notice pay in lieu: permissible — pay gross for notice days not served

FULL & FINAL SETTLEMENT CHECKLIST:
□ Last month salary calculated (pro-rated if mid-month exit)
□ Leave encashment (EL balance × daily rate)
□ Gratuity (if 5 years completed)
□ Bonus (if applicable under Payment of Bonus Act — firms with 20+ employees)
□ Reimbursement of outstanding expenses
□ Recovery: notice pay shortfall, advances, laptop/asset deductions (as per contract)
□ Form 16 issued within 15 days of end of financial year (or on request)
□ PF transfer/withdrawal: assist employee with Form 13 (transfer) or Form 19/10C (withdrawal)
□ Relieving letter: issued within 15 days of last working day
□ Experience letter: issued simultaneously with relieving letter

IMPORTANT: Withholding salary/relieving letter to coerce repayment is illegal. If recovery is due, initiate civil proceedings.

SECTION 6: POSH COMPLIANCE (Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace Act, 2013)

APPLICABILITY: All employers with 10+ employees
MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS:
1. Internal Committee (IC): constitute within 90 days of Act applicability
   - Chairperson: senior woman employee
   - 2 other employees (sensitivity to women's issues)
   - 1 external member (NGO / women's rights background)
   - Term: 3 years

2. Policy: written sexual harassment policy; circulate to all employees annually

3. Awareness: workshop / training at least once per year

4. Annual report: IC submits report to District Officer (Form with state authority) by 31 January
   - Report includes: cases received, disposed, pending, training conducted

PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE:
- First offence: fine up to ₹50,000
- Subsequent: doubled + cancellation of registration/licence

BEST PRACTICE: Even firms below 10 employees should constitute a Local Committee (LC) via district administration and adopt a written policy.
