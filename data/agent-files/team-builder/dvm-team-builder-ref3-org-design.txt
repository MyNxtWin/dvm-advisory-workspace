SECTION 1: SPAN OF CONTROL GUIDELINES

PROFESSIONAL SERVICES CONTEXT:
- Partners: manage 2–4 Managers directly (beyond 4, quality of supervision degrades)
- Managers: supervise 3–6 Executives (file complexity determines upper end)
- Executives: may guide 1–2 trainees with structured task allocation

SIGNALS THAT SPAN IS TOO WIDE:
- Partners spending >40% of time in execution (not supervision/advisory)
- Managers unable to review all deliverables before client dispatch
- Trainees left without daily check-in
- Error rates trending up without identifiable root cause

SIGNALS THAT STRUCTURE IS TOO FLAT:
- Executives escalating directly to Partners, bypassing Managers
- Managers doing work that trained Executives could handle
- Decision bottlenecks at senior levels slowing file closure

SECTION 2: ESOP DESIGN FOR TEAM RETENTION

ESOP IS THE STRONGEST LONG-TERM RETENTION TOOL FOR GROWING FIRMS.

BASIC STRUCTURE UNDER COMPANIES ACT, 2013 (Section 62(1)(b)):
- Approved by shareholders (special resolution)
- Administered by Compensation/NRC Committee
- Options granted at Exercise Price (typically FMV or discounted)
- Vesting schedule: industry standard is 4 years with 1-year cliff
  → 25% vests at end of Year 1; 6.25% per quarter thereafter

TYPICAL TERMS FOR ADVISORY FIRM:
- Pool size: 5–15% of paid-up capital
- Eligible: Tier 2 and above; discretionary grants for exceptional Tier 3 performers
- Exercise window: options exercisable within 5 years of vesting; lapse on exit
- Acceleration: full vesting on change of control (standard market protection)

TAX IMPLICATIONS FOR EMPLOYEE:
- Perquisite tax at exercise: FMV on exercise date – exercise price = taxable perquisite (added to salary)
  → Employer deducts TDS on this amount
  → For unlisted companies: FMV = SEBI prescribed valuation method (DCF/NAV by SEBI-registered valuer)
- Capital gains on sale: sale price – FMV at exercise = STCG (if held <24 months) or LTCG
  → LTCG on unlisted shares: 20% with indexation if >24 months

ESOP TRUST vs DIRECT GRANT:
- Direct grant: simpler; firm holds treasury shares or issues fresh shares on exercise
- ESOP Trust: separate trust holds shares; better for large pool; requires trust deed, trustee appointment

COMMUNICATION TO EMPLOYEES:
- Grant letter: individual letter with number of options, exercise price, vesting schedule
- Hypothetical value illustration: "If company value doubles, your options are worth ₹X" — motivates
- Annual statement: outstanding vested/unvested options, lapse risk on exit

SECTION 3: JOB DESCRIPTIONS — STANDARD TEMPLATES

---
JD: COMPANY SECRETARY — MANAGER
---
ROLE TITLE: Manager — Corporate Secretarial
REPORTS TO: Partner / Senior Manager
LOCATION: [City] | Hybrid

PURPOSE:
Lead end-to-end secretarial compliance for a portfolio of 10–15 clients across listed and unlisted entities.

KEY RESPONSIBILITIES:
1. Manage all MCA filings: annual returns (MGT-7, AOC-4), event-based filings (PAS-3, SH-7, MGT-14, CHG-1), director KYC (DIR-3 KYC), DIN-related matters
2. Draft and finalise board meeting notices, agendas, and minutes in compliance with SS-1; AGM/EGM notices and minutes per SS-2
3. SEBI LODR compliance for listed clients: Regulation 29/30 intimations, quarterly filings, annual corporate governance report
4. FEMA/RBI: FC-GPR, FC-TRS, FLA, APR filings for clients with foreign investment
5. Supervise and review work of 2–3 Executives in pod; conduct weekly file reviews
6. Client communications: primary contact for secretarial matters; attend board meetings as required
7. Stay current with MCA amendments, SEBI circulars, ICSI pronouncements; brief team quarterly

QUALIFICATIONS:
- ACS (Associate Member of ICSI) — mandatory
- 3–6 years post-qualification experience in a CA/CS firm or in-house CS team
- Proven MCA21 V3 and SEBI LODR filing experience
- Listed company experience preferred

COMPETENCIES:
- Regulatory precision with zero tolerance for filing errors
- Client communication — clear, professional, confident
- Deadline discipline — manages multiple concurrent deadlines without prompting
- Mentoring — demonstrated ability to develop junior team members

JD: EXECUTIVE — CORPORATE SECRETARIAL
---
ROLE TITLE: Executive — Corporate Secretarial
REPORTS TO: Manager
LOCATION: [City] | Hybrid

PURPOSE:
Execute secretarial filings and documentation across a portfolio of assigned clients under Manager supervision.

KEY RESPONSIBILITIES:
1. Prepare and file MCA forms: AOC-4, MGT-7, PAS-3, SH-7, CHG-1, DIR-3 KYC, INC-22A
2. Maintain statutory registers: Members Register, Directors Register, Debenture Register, Charge Register
3. Draft notices and agenda items for board and committee meetings under Manager guidance
4. Prepare board meeting packs: compile agenda, resolutions, supporting documents
5. Track compliance calendar for assigned clients; flag upcoming deadlines 15 days in advance
6. Correspondence: draft letters to ROC, SEBI, stock exchanges as directed

QUALIFICATIONS:
- CS Final (passed or attempting) or CS Executive (Passed) with 1–3 years experience
- Hands-on MCA21 V3 filing experience mandatory
- Basic knowledge of Companies Act, 2013 — Sections 92, 117, 137, 152–168, 184

COMPETENCIES:
- Detail orientation — zero filing errors
- Time management — multiple deadlines simultaneously
- Learning mindset — proactively reads MCA/SEBI updates
- Communication — clear written and verbal in English

SECTION 4: CULTURE & VALUES FRAMEWORK

For a professional advisory firm, culture is built around four pillars:

PILLAR 1 — PRECISION
- Zero-error filing is the non-negotiable standard
- Peer review before every client dispatch
- Error post-mortems: when an error occurs, root-cause analysis in 48 hours; outcome shared with team (anonymised) within a week

PILLAR 2 — OWNERSHIP
- "I own this file" mentality — not "I was assigned this task"
- Ownership extends to client calls, not just documents
- Escalation culture: escalate early, not after the deadline has passed

PILLAR 3 — CONTINUOUS LEARNING
- Every regulatory change is a learning opportunity
- Knowledge sharing: 15-minute team briefing every fortnight on one significant amendment
- Study leave and exam support signals that the firm invests in its people

PILLAR 4 — INTEGRITY
- Confidentiality is absolute — client data never discussed outside secure channels
- Conflict of interest disclosed upfront; escalated to Partner if uncertain
- No shortcuts on compliance — if a client wants to cut corners, it is escalated, not accommodated

CULTURE RED FLAGS TO MONITOR:
- "That's not my job" responses between team members
- Hiding errors rather than reporting them
- Client complaints about responsiveness (response time standard: within 4 business hours)
- High attrition in a single pod (signals a manager issue, not a market issue)

SECTION 5: TEAM MEETING CADENCE

DAILY STANDUP (10 mins, all hands in pod):
- What did I complete yesterday?
- What am I working on today?
- Any blockers?

WEEKLY TEAM REVIEW (30 mins, Manager + Executives):
- Open files status: on track / at risk / overdue
- Client feedback received this week
- Regulatory update of the week (1 item, 5 mins)

MONTHLY PARTNER REVIEW (60 mins, Partner + all Managers):
- Client portfolio health
- Upcoming high-stakes filings (AGMs, LODR deadlines)
- Team capacity and hiring needs
- Learning: one training topic per month

QUARTERLY ALL-HANDS (90 mins, entire team):
- Business update from Partners
- Top performer recognition
- HR update: appraisal cycle, policy changes
- Team-building activity (30 mins)

SECTION 6: SUCCESSION PLANNING

IDENTIFY KEY-PERSON DEPENDENCIES:
- List every client → who is the primary contact?
- If that person left tomorrow: who picks up, and are they ready?
- Target: no client should have a single point of failure

SUCCESSION MATRIX (update annually):
ROLE | INCUMBENT | SUCCESSOR (READY NOW) | SUCCESSOR (6–12 MONTHS) | GAP
Partner | A | None | B (Senior Manager) | B needs client ownership experience
Manager | C | D (Executive) | — | D needs SEBI LODR exposure

KNOWLEDGE TRANSFER PROTOCOL:
- Every Manager maintains a "client dossier": key contacts, open matters, filing calendar, quirks
- Dossier stored centrally (not on personal laptop)
- Updated whenever a significant client event occurs
- Reviewed by Partner quarterly
