DVM ADVISORY — TEAM BUILDING REFERENCE
HIRING FRAMEWORK FOR PROFESSIONAL SERVICES FIRMS
=================================================

SOURCE: DVM Internal HR Practice Guide v2.1

---

SECTION 1: ROLE DEFINITION FRAMEWORK

For CS/CA/Legal Advisory Firms, roles fall into four tiers:

TIER 1 — PARTNER / DIRECTOR LEVEL
- Minimum qualification: ACS/FCS or FCA with 10+ years PQE
- Responsibilities: Client relationships, signing authority, practice leadership
- KPIs: Billable hours (target 1,200/year), client retention rate, new mandate wins
- Compensation band: ₹40L–₹1.2 Cr CTC depending on city and specialisation
- Notice period: 3–6 months (non-negotiable for client continuity)

TIER 2 — MANAGER / ASSOCIATE LEVEL
- Qualification: ACS/ACA/LLB with 3–7 years experience
- Responsibilities: File management, client deliverables, junior team oversight
- KPIs: File closure rate, deadline adherence, client escalations, review pass rate
- Compensation band: ₹8L–₹25L CTC
- Notice period: 60 days standard

TIER 3 — EXECUTIVE / ASSOCIATE LEVEL
- Qualification: CS/CA Inter or Final (attempting), BCom/LLB
- Responsibilities: Documentation, filings, research, data management
- KPIs: Turnaround time, error rate, MCA/SEBI portal proficiency
- Compensation band: ₹3L–₹8L CTC
- Notice period: 30 days

TIER 4 — TRAINEE / ARTICLESHIP
- Qualification: CS/CA enrolled student
- Stipend: ₹3,000–₹8,000/month (ICSI prescribed minimum: ₹2,000 for first year)
- Duration: 15 months (ICSI) or 3 years (ICAI articleship)
- Conversion rate target: 60% trainees offered full-time post qualification

SECTION 2: INTERVIEW STRUCTURE — PROFESSIONAL SERVICES

ROUND 1 — TECHNICAL SCREENING (30 mins, HR/Senior Executive)
- Domain knowledge test: 15 MCQs (Companies Act, SEBI, GST basics)
- File drafting sample: given one scenario, produce a notice or resolution draft
- Portal familiarity: MCA21, SEBI SCORES, TRACES, GST portal

ROUND 2 — CASE STUDY (45 mins, Manager level)
- Scenario: "A listed company's AGM date is 15 September. The financial year ended 31 March. Auditor's report has a qualification. Draft the audit committee agenda, list the LODR filings required, and identify three compliance risks."
- Evaluation: Process thinking, regulatory accuracy, communication clarity

ROUND 3 — PARTNER INTERVIEW (30 mins, cultural fit + client-facing readiness)
- Situational questions on deadline pressure, client communication, error correction
- Assessment: maturity, ownership mindset, long-term commitment signal

BACKGROUND VERIFICATION CHECKLIST:
□ Educational certificates (ICSI/ICAI membership number verified online)
□ Previous employment — offer letter + relieving letter
□ Reference call: 1 professional + 1 academic
□ Address verification (Aadhaar/Passport)
□ Criminal record check (for roles with signing authority)

SECTION 3: ONBOARDING CHECKLIST (Day 1–30)

DAY 1
□ Laptop/device issued; email ID created; system access provisioned
□ NDA and confidentiality agreement signed
□ Introduction to team, buddy assigned
□ Overview of active client roster (under NDA)

WEEK 1
□ Shadowing senior on 2–3 live files
□ Read firm's standard operating procedures (SOPs)
□ Practice portal access: MCA21, SEBI SCORES, BSE/NSE listing portal, TRACES
□ 1-on-1 with direct manager: goals for first 90 days set

WEEK 2–4
□ First independent file assigned (low-risk, supervised)
□ Mid-onboarding check-in with HR
□ Compliance calendar for current month explained
□ Introduction to firm's billing and time-tracking system

DAY 30
□ 30-day review: self-assessment + manager assessment
□ Confirm notice period, leave policy, insurance enrollment

SECTION 4: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK

QUARTERLY REVIEW PARAMETERS (for Executives and above):
1. Deadline adherence — % of files closed on or before due date (target: 95%)
2. Error rate — client complaints or rework requests / total files (target: <3%)
3. Learning — certifications attempted, CPE hours, internal training
4. Team contribution — peer feedback score (1–5 scale)
5. Client-readiness — partner assessment of client-facing skill progression

ANNUAL APPRAISAL CYCLE:
- Self-assessment submitted by March 31
- Manager assessment by April 15
- Partner calibration session: April 20–25
- Letters issued: May 1
- Increment effective: April 1 (backdated)

PROMOTION CRITERIA:
- Tier 3 → Tier 2: Minimum 2 years, ACS/ACA qualification obtained, avg quarterly score ≥4.0/5.0
- Tier 2 → Tier 1: Minimum 4 years at Tier 2, client ownership demonstrated, partner recommendation

PIP (PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLAN):
- Triggered if two consecutive quarterly scores below 2.5/5.0
- Duration: 60 days
- Success criteria defined upfront in writing
- Exit options: reassignment, mutual separation, termination per appointment letter terms

SECTION 5: RETENTION TOOLKIT

MONETARY
- Retention bonus: 10–15% of annual CTC, payable after 2-year lock-in from date of issue
- ESOP: Applicable to Tier 2 and above; typical vesting: 4-year cliff-vest (25% per year)
- Performance bonus: quarterly, capped at 20% of base

NON-MONETARY
- Exam support: paid study leave (5 days per attempt), exam fee reimbursement
- Conference sponsorship: 1 industry conference per year for Tier 2+
- Flexible work: hybrid model (3 days office mandatory for Tier 3, 2 days for Tier 2+)
- Title progression: mid-year title upgrade possible without salary change (recognition signal)

EXIT INTERVIEW PROTOCOL:
- Conducted by HR, not direct manager
- Structured questionnaire: reason for leaving, salary, growth, culture, workload
- Data aggregated quarterly to identify attrition patterns
- Feedback shared with partners anonymously in quarterly HR review

SECTION 6: TEAM STRUCTURE MODELS FOR CS/CA FIRMS

MODEL A — FUNCTIONAL PODS (suitable for 10–30 staff)
- Each pod: 1 Manager + 2 Executives + 1 Trainee
- Pod specialisation: listed/unlisted, transaction, tax
- Advantage: deep expertise, clear ownership
- Risk: siloing; cross-training required quarterly

MODEL B — CLIENT-ALIGNED TEAMS (suitable for 30+ staff)
- Each client assigned a primary Manager + backup
- Executives rotate across clients
- Advantage: strong client relationships
- Risk: key-person dependency; mitigate with documented knowledge base

MODEL C — HYBRID (recommended for growth phase)
- Core specialisation pods + shared execution pool
- Execution pool: junior executives rotated based on load
- Advantage: flexibility + specialisation
- Risk: requires strong project management discipline
